Public Transports in Eastern Australia

Short Platform Availability

Across Australia's public transport network, short platforms are key in shaping station capacity. There are 175 facilities with short platforms, mostly in space-constrained areas that can't accommodate longer trains. This impacts service frequency train capacity, and overall system efficiency.

Short platforms, while needed in some areas, can create bottlenecks in high-demand regions. Upgrading them is key to meeting Australia's growing transport needs.

New South Wales vs Victoria

New South Wales has 175 short platform facilities, highlighting potential capacity issues in the state. In contrast, Victoria has no short platforms, reflecting a different infrastructure approach to managing capacity and transport services.

New South Wales also features a more diverse transport network, with 263 coach facilities and various combinations of train, bus, and ferry services. Victoria, however, has fewer facilities focused primarily on regional coach and train services.

Seasonal Trends and Holiday Impact

January consistently shows lower public transport patronage across multiple modes, particularly for the Train. This trend is likely influenced by the holiday season, as many people are on vacation or not working during this time. This pattern is also reflected in the December numbers, which are relatively lower due to the lead-up to the holiday period.

Post-COVID Recovery

From 2021 to 2023, there is a noticeable increase in patronage across all transport modes, including the Train and Regional coach, after the sharp decline seen in 2020 during the pandemic lockdowns. For instance, by July 2023, the patronage for Metropolitan train had risen to 14 million, showing a strong recovery as restrictions eased and people returned to regular commuting.

Staff Complaints (2023)

One major observation from the visualization is the significant spike in complaints about Behaviour of staff in 2023. This category saw a steep surge, suggesting a rise in tensions between staff and passengers. This could be related to increasing commuter frustrations or the challenges of post-pandemic service recovery. Addressing staff training and improving passenger interactions should be a high priority to improve the overall transport experience.

The rise may also reflect higher passenger expectations post-pandemic. As regular commuting resumes, passengers likely expect better professionalism and service from staff. Improving customer service could help reduce future complaints.

Rise in Punctuality Complaints

Complaints related to Punctuality have seen a gradual rise from 2016 to 2023, indicating persistent issues with delays and unreliable services. The steady increase in complaints about punctuality highlights the need for better scheduling, clearer communication with passengers during delays, and potential infrastructure upgrades to ensure more reliable service delivery.

Tram Network Insights

The tram network in Victoria and New South Wales is an essential mode of urban transport, especially in densely populated areas. This map shows the key routes in both states, with a focus on major tram lines that serve busy city centers and surrounding suburbs. Victoria, particularly Melbourne, is renowned for having one of the largest tram networks in the world, facilitating millions of daily commuters.

The visualization provides insights into how trams play a role in reducing traffic congestion by offering an efficient alternative to buses and cars, while also being a sustainable transport option. The key starting and ending points in this map highlight the extent of tram service reach and its impact
